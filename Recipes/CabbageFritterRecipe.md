Cabbage Fritters
================
Most generally, fritters are a bunch of stuff, usually vegitable-heavy, mixed with _just_ enough unsweetened pancake batter to _barely_ hold together when fried.

They're _great_ for fridge cleaning - leftovers that aren't enough for a meal plus the sad but still ok veggies you've been meaning to use up, etc.

For my cabbage fritters:
- 1/4 head of cabbage, chopped (as ribbons can help the fritter hold together)
- 2-3 rashers of bacon, cooked and chopped (optional)

- 100g flour
- 100g liquid (can be water, milk, broth, ...)
    - *or* 200g of sourdough discard
- 50ml egg (one "grade A large")
- 2.5g salt
- 1-2g baking powder

- herbs de provance
- black pepper

Mix batter and seasonings, then add filling and mix to thoroughly coat; it won't look like enough at first, but it usually is.  You _may_ have to adjust if you have a particularly large or small cabbage.

Fry flattened scoops in a skillet with a little fat (butter, bacon drippings, olive oil, whatever); flip when you can see the batter setting up most of the way up the side.

Enjoy with butter, salt, cider or rice vinegar, seasoned aioli, ...
